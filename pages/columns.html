Userland columns, not browser columns.

This spread uses a single `layoutWithLines()` call, then repartitions the resulting lines into three columns in userland. Resize the spread and the article reflows without asking the browser to decide where the columns break.

The point is not to replace every CSS text primitive. The point is that once lines are explicit, higher-level editorial layout stops being a one-way trip into block layout. This is the sort of thing that becomes tractable once the line model is ours.
